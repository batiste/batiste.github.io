Gameplay Preview

In DeckHand, players take turns accumulating gold coins and purchasing new cards to enhance their decks and increase their power. Competing against opponents, you'll aim to acquire the most valuable cards, strategically building synergies and thwarting your opponents' plans. Speed is of the essence as you strive to execute your strategy before your opponents, all while racing against the clock to achieve victory.

In order to get a sense of what to expect in DeckHand, explore a subset of the 59 cards showcasing intriguing facets of the game.

Understanding the Rules

Rules should be concise and clear, leaving no room for ambiguity.

Simplified Game for Beginners

To set up a beginner game, you may exclude up to 11 cards for a 2 to 3 player game. No cards can be excluded for a 4-player game. Feel free to remove any cards you find complex or that might slow down the game. Here is a suggested list: The Grand Council, Gold Mine, Emperor's Caprice, Imperial Powder, Typhoon, Monkey's Bargain, Governor's Wedding, Kraken's Fury, Ghost Crew, End of the Rainbow, Going Ape.

Board

This serves as an illustrative board state for a single player. It's essential that each player organizes their stash/discard pile in a visually clear and easily readable manner.

Solo Challenges

Explore the game in Solo! Sharpen your skills and knowledge of the cards while taking on these challenges individually.
During Solo challenges, you take charge of making decisions that enemies would typically make.
If an effect targets an enemy, the targeting aspect of the effect is disregarded, while other parts of the effect still take place.

Victory Beast Solo Challenge

Create a 23-card supply deck. At the start of your turn remove one card from the supply from the game. Aim to finish the game with at least 32 victory points.

Attack Freak Solo Challenge

Create a 23-card supply deck. At the start of your turn remove one card from the supply from the game. Aim to finish the game with at least 26 victory points and at least 9 Attacks.

Architect Solo Challenge

Create a 23-card supply deck. At the start of your turn remove one card from the supply from the game. Aim to finish the game with at least 27 victory points and at least 8 Structure.
